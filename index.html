About Me

Computer Science student at Rutgers University with a focus on Systems Architecture.

I have always gravitated toward the "hard" parts of computing. While many developers stay in high-level abstractions, I feel most at home with C, Assembly, and memory management. I want to understand the machine at its lowest level—where the software meets the hardware.

However, technical skill means nothing without perspective. My coursework in Minds, Machines, & Persons forces me to step back and ask the human questions: How do we build systems that are not just efficient, but ethically sound and user-centric?

I thrive in collaborative environments because I don't just want to be the "smartest" person in the room; I want to be the most helpful teammate.
